# PRD Question Bank

## Foundation Questions (Phase 2)

1. **Who** has this problem? Be specific — not "users" but what type of person/role.
2. **What** problem are they facing? Observable pain, not assumed need.
3. **Why** can't they solve it today? What alternatives exist and why do they fail?
4. **Why now?** What changed that makes this worth building?
5. **How** will you know if you solved it? What would success look like?

## Deep Dive Questions (Phase 4)

1. **Vision**: In one sentence, what's the ideal end state if this succeeds wildly?
2. **Primary User**: Describe your most important user — role, context, trigger.
3. **Job to Be Done**: "When [situation], I want to [motivation], so I can [outcome]."
4. **Non-Users**: Who is explicitly NOT the target? Who should we ignore?
5. **Constraints**: Time, budget, technical, regulatory limitations?

## Decisions Questions (Phase 6)

1. **MVP Definition**: Absolute minimum to test if this works?
2. **Must Have vs Nice to Have**: 2-3 things that MUST be in v1? What can wait?
3. **Key Hypothesis**: "We believe [capability] will [solve problem] for [users]. We'll know we're right when [measurable outcome]."
4. **Out of Scope**: What are you explicitly NOT building (even if users ask)?
5. **Open Questions**: What uncertainties could change the approach?
